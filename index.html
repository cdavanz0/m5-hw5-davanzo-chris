The Weeknd Artist Page

Early Life

The Weeknd was born Abel Makkonen Tesfaye on February 16, 1990 in Toronto, Ontario, Canada. His culutral background is Ethopian. Born to father Makkonen Tesfaye and mother Samrawit Hailu, his parents seperated shortly after his birth, leading him to be raised by his mother and grandmother. Abel attended schools West Hills Collegiate Institute and Birchmount Park Collegiate Instittue in Toronto, learning to speak fluent French. At age 17, Abel dropped out of school and began releasing music on YouTube.

Career

The Weeknd adopted the stage name in 2010 and released several mixtapes between 2010 and 2011. His first studio album, titled "Trilogy", released in 2012 was a compilation of songs from his previous three mixtapes. In 2015, the song "The Hills" reached number one on the Billboard Hot 100 chart, bringing new notority as a mainstream pop sensation. That year, several of his songs were featured in the film Fifty Shades of Gray, and his album Beauty Behind the Madness debuted at number one. In 2016, the Weeknd released the album "Starboy", which was the second consecutive album to debut at number one on the Billboard Hot 200 chart for records. The Weeknd has since released two more hit studio albums, "After Hours" in 2020 and "Dawn FM" in 2022.

Other Interests

As an actor, The Weeknd has appeared in several films and television shows. He was a co-writer and guest star on a 2020 episode of American Dad, as well as been a guest star on The Simpsons. He has appeared as a host and musical guest of Saturday Night Live and played himself in the 2019 film Uncut Gems. In addtion to acting, The Weeknd has several business and philantropic ventures. As a fashion influencer, he's parterned with the company H&M to develop a men's clothing line. He has also contributed money to build black owned businesses, build hospitals in Uganda, and fight COVID-19 in Canada.
